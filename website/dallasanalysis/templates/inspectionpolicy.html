Geo Inspection Policy

Inspection Frequency

Restaurants are inspected at least twice a year, with more frequent inspections for establishments that have previously failed or have a history of violations.

Grading Scale

Inspection scores range from 0 to 100:

  • 90-100: Very good
  • 80-89: Satisfactory but requires improvements
  • 70-79: Marginal, needing significant improvements and more frequent follow-ups
  • 60-69: Failing, requires re-inspection within 10 days
  • Below 60: Immediate closure until critical violations are resolved

Violation Types

Violations are categorized into three levels based on severity:

  • Critical Violations: Direct threats to public health; require immediate attention.
  • Non-Critical Violations: Less severe, but still need corrective actions.
  • Minor Violations: Least severe, with minimal impact on health but need addressing.

Violation Score System

Each violation results in a specific point deduction based on severity:

  • Critical Violation Example: Presence of pests or improper food handling temperatures (4-5 points deduction).
  • Non-Critical Violation Example: Improper labeling or minor sanitation issues (2-3 points deduction).
  • Minor Violation Example: Slightly cluttered storage or minor documentation issues (1 point deduction).

Impact on Overall Score

The overall inspection score starts at 100, with deductions based on violations:

  • Score 90-100: Very good, follow-up inspections every six months.
  • Score 80-89: Satisfactory but needs improvements.
  • Score 70-79: Marginal, requiring more frequent follow-ups.
  • Score 60-69: Failing, requiring re-inspection within 10 days.
  • Below 60: Immediate closure until critical issues are resolved.
